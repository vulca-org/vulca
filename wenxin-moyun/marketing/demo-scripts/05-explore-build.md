# Demo Script #5: Explore & Build — Cultural Knowledge Base (30s)

> **Duration**: 30 seconds
> **Mode**: Canvas → Explore → Build
> **Audience**: Cultural researchers, art educators, creative AI users
> **Key Message**: Browse existing cultural traditions, then build your own custom tradition with YAML export

---

## Pre-Recording Checklist

- [ ] Open https://vulcaart.art in Chrome (1920×1080, 100% zoom)
- [ ] Navigate to Canvas page → switch to Explore mode
- [ ] Ensure 8 tradition cards are loaded (Chinese Xieyi, Japanese Ukiyo-e, Korean Minhwa, Islamic Geometric, Indian Miniature, Western Classical, Western Modern, African)
- [ ] Screen recorder: 1080p, 30fps minimum
- [ ] Browser bookmarks bar hidden

---

## Shot-by-Shot Script

### [0:00–0:03] Opening — Explore Mode Grid

**Screen**: Explore mode — 8 tradition cards in a 2×4 or 4×2 grid layout, each with tradition name, icon, and brief description

**Action**: None (hold shot, slight scroll if needed to show all 8)

**Annotation**: `VULCA Explore — 8 Cultural Traditions` (top-left)

**Voice-over cue**: "Eight cultural traditions. Hundreds of terms. All explorable."

---

### [0:03–0:06] Expand a Tradition Card

**Screen**: Chinese Xieyi card expands

**Action**:
1. (0:03) Click "Chinese Xieyi" card
2. (0:04–0:06) Card expands to reveal:
   - **Key Terms**: 留白 (liúbái/negative space), 气韵 (qìyùn/spirit resonance), 墨分五色 (five shades of ink)
   - **Taboos/Constraints**: Avoid photorealistic rendering, respect seal placement conventions
   - **Weight distribution**: L1-L5 bar chart showing emphasis on L3 (Context) and L4 (Meaning)

**Annotation**:
- Arrow to terms: `Cultural terminology`
- Arrow to taboos: `Tradition-specific constraints`

**Voice-over cue**: "Chinese Xieyi — with its key terms, cultural constraints, and evaluation weights."

---

### [0:06–0:10] Cross-Tradition Search

**Screen**: Search bar at top of Explore mode

**Action**:
1. (0:06–0:07) Click search box
2. (0:07–0:09) Type `space` (for "negative space" / "spatial composition")
3. (0:09–0:10) Results appear across multiple traditions:
   - Chinese Xieyi: 留白 (liúbái) — negative space as active element
   - Japanese Ukiyo-e: 間 (ma) — interval/pause
   - Islamic Geometric: void patterns — mathematical negative space
   - Western Modern: white space — compositional breath

**Annotation**: `Search "space" → results span 4+ traditions`

**Voice-over cue**: "Search any concept — see how different traditions interpret it. 'Space' means something different in every culture."

---

### [0:10–0:14] Multi-Tradition Radar Comparison

**Screen**: Tradition comparison view

**Action**:
1. (0:10–0:11) Check/select "Chinese Xieyi" checkbox
2. (0:11–0:12) Check/select "Japanese Ukiyo-e" checkbox
3. (0:12–0:13) Check/select "Islamic Geometric" checkbox
4. (0:13–0:14) Comparison radar chart appears with 3 overlapping polygons

**Annotation**: `Select traditions → Compare weight profiles`

**Voice-over cue**: "Select any traditions. Compare their evaluation profiles side by side."

---

### [0:14–0:17] Radar Chart Detail

**Screen**: Radar chart with 3 tradition polygons

**Action**:
1. (0:14–0:15) Hover over L2 (Technique) axis — tooltip shows:
   - Chinese Xieyi: 0.20
   - Japanese Ukiyo-e: 0.25
   - Islamic Geometric: 0.30
2. (0:15–0:17) Hover over L3 (Context) axis — tooltip shows:
   - Chinese Xieyi: 0.30
   - Japanese Ukiyo-e: 0.22
   - Islamic Geometric: 0.25

**Annotation**: `Different traditions weight different dimensions`

**Voice-over cue**: "Each tradition weights dimensions differently. Islamic art emphasizes technique. Chinese Xieyi emphasizes context."

---

### [0:17–0:20] Switch to Build Mode

**Screen**: Mode switcher → Build

**Action**:
1. (0:17–0:18) Click "Build" tab in mode selector
2. (0:18–0:20) TraditionBuilder interface appears:
   - Name field (empty)
   - L1-L5 weight sliders (default position)
   - Terminology section (empty)
   - Taboo/constraint section (empty)
   - Pipeline variant selector
   - YAML preview panel (right side)

**Annotation**: `Build Mode — Create your own tradition`

**Voice-over cue**: "Now build your own."

---

### [0:20–0:24] Fill in Custom Tradition

**Screen**: TraditionBuilder form

**Action** (fast but visible):
1. (0:20–0:21) Type name: `my_fusion_style`
2. (0:21–0:22) Drag L3 (Context) slider to 0.35 (high priority)
3. (0:22–0:23) Drag L5 (Innovation) slider to 0.30
4. (0:23–0:24) Drag L2 (Technique) slider down to 0.10

**Annotation**:
- On name: `Name your tradition`
- On sliders: `Set evaluation priorities`

**Voice-over cue**: "Name it. Set your priorities — high context, high innovation, relaxed technique."

---

### [0:24–0:27] Add Terminology & Constraints

**Screen**: TraditionBuilder — terms and taboos sections

**Action**:
1. (0:24–0:25) Click "Add Term" → type `cultural_fusion` → Enter
2. (0:25–0:26) Click "Add Term" → type `cross_tradition_dialogue` → Enter
3. (0:26–0:27) Click "Add Taboo" → type `monocultural_stereotypes` → Enter

**Annotation**: `Define what matters (and what doesn't)`

**Voice-over cue**: "Add your terminology. Define your constraints."

---

### [0:27–0:29] Preview YAML

**Screen**: YAML preview panel (right side of Build mode)

**Action**:
1. (0:27–0:28) Click "Preview YAML" button
2. (0:28–0:29) YAML content renders:
```yaml
name: my_fusion_style
weights:
  recognition: 0.10
  technique: 0.10
  context: 0.35
  meaning: 0.15
  innovation: 0.30
terminology:
  - cultural_fusion
  - cross_tradition_dialogue
taboos:
  - monocultural_stereotypes
pipeline_variant: default
```

**Annotation**: `Live YAML preview`

**Voice-over cue**: "Live YAML preview — exactly what the evaluation engine will use."

---

### [0:29–0:30] Download & Close

**Screen**: YAML preview + download button

**Action**: Click "Download .yaml" button → file save dialog flashes briefly

**Annotation**:
- Center: `Browse. Build. Download.`
- Bottom: `vulcaart.art` + `Open Source`

**Voice-over cue**: "Browse eight traditions. Build your own. Download as YAML. That's VULCA."

# Demo Script #3: Compare — Cross-Cultural Art Evaluation (40s)

> **Duration**: 40 seconds
> **Mode**: Canvas → Compare
> **Audience**: Art historians, cultural researchers, AI ethics teams, general audience
> **Key Message**: Same artwork, different cultures, completely different scores — proving why one-size-fits-all evaluation fails

---

## Pre-Recording Checklist

- [ ] Open https://vulcaart.art in Chrome (1920×1080, 100% zoom)
- [ ] Navigate to Canvas page → switch to Compare mode
- [ ] Download demo images first: `cd marketing/demo-assets && bash download.sh`
- [ ] Verify 3 images exist in `demo-assets/images/`:
  - `01-dunhuang-flying-apsaras.jpg`
  - `02-hokusai-great-wave.jpg`
  - `03-met-mihrab-geometric.jpg`
- [ ] Have the 3 images ready in a Finder/Explorer window, easily draggable
- [ ] Screen recorder: 1080p, 30fps minimum

---

## Shot-by-Shot Script

### [0:00–0:03] Opening — Compare Mode Empty State

**Screen**: Compare mode — large drop zone with dashed border, text reads "Drop 2–6 images to compare"

**Action**: None (hold shot)

**Annotation**: `VULCA Compare Mode` (top-left)

**Voice-over cue**: "Same artwork. Three cultures. Completely different scores."

---

### [0:03–0:08] Drag & Drop Three Artworks

**Screen**: File explorer visible on side → Compare mode drop zone

**Action**:
1. (0:03–0:04) Drag `01-dunhuang-flying-apsaras.jpg` into drop zone → card appears with thumbnail
2. (0:05–0:06) Drag `02-hokusai-great-wave.jpg` → second card appears
3. (0:07–0:08) Drag `03-met-mihrab-geometric.jpg` → third card appears

**Annotation** (labels appear with each drop):
- Card 1: `Dunhuang · Chinese Buddhist`
- Card 2: `Hokusai · Japanese Ukiyo-e`
- Card 3: `Mihrab · Islamic Geometric`

**Voice-over cue**: "Three masterpieces. Three traditions. Drop them in."

---

### [0:08–0:12] Evaluation Begins

**Screen**: Three image cards with "Evaluating..." status

**Action**:
- Each card shows a pulsing blue progress indicator
- Cards pulse in sequence (slight stagger):
  - Card 1: Pulsing... (0:08)
  - Card 2: Pulsing... (0:09)
  - Card 3: Pulsing... (0:10)

**Annotation**: `Analyzing across L1–L5 dimensions...`

**Voice-over cue**: "VULCA evaluates each artwork across five cultural dimensions."

---

### [0:12–0:20] Scores Fill In

**Screen**: Score bars and badges appear on each card

**Action** (scores populate progressively):
- (0:12–0:14) **Dunhuang** scores fill:
  - L1 Recognition: 89
  - L2 Technique: 76
  - L3 Context: 94
  - L4 Meaning: 91
  - L5 Innovation: 68

- (0:14–0:16) **Hokusai** scores fill:
  - L1 Recognition: 95
  - L2 Technique: 92
  - L3 Context: 85
  - L4 Meaning: 78
  - L5 Innovation: 88

- (0:16–0:18) **Mihrab** scores fill:
  - L1 Recognition: 72
  - L2 Technique: 96
  - L3 Context: 91
  - L4 Meaning: 84
  - L5 Innovation: 65

- (0:18–0:20) Brief pause to absorb the numbers

**Annotation**: Highlight the biggest variance: `Context: 94 vs 85 vs 91 — culture matters`

**Voice-over cue**: "Watch the scores. Same evaluation framework — dramatically different profiles."

---

### [0:20–0:28] Comparison Radar Chart

**Screen**: Radar chart expands — 3 overlapping polygons in distinct colors

**Action**:
1. (0:20–0:22) Radar chart animates in — axes labeled L1-L5
2. (0:22–0:24) Three polygons draw simultaneously:
   - **Red polygon**: Dunhuang (high Context, high Meaning)
   - **Blue polygon**: Hokusai (high Technique, high Innovation)
   - **Green polygon**: Mihrab (high Technique, high Context)
3. (0:24–0:26) Hover over the L5 (Innovation) axis → tooltip shows max gap
4. (0:26–0:28) Brief hold — the three completely different shapes tell the story

**Annotation**:
- Arrow pointing to shape difference: `Three cultures, three shapes`
- Below chart: `One score can never capture this`

**Voice-over cue**: "Three polygons. Three completely different shapes. This is why one score tells you nothing."

---

### [0:28–0:34] Difference Table

**Screen**: Table view showing per-dimension comparison

**Action**:
1. (0:28–0:30) Scroll to or click "Table" tab
2. (0:30–0:32) Table reveals:
   - Rows: L1, L2, L3, L4, L5
   - Columns: Dunhuang, Hokusai, Mihrab, **Max Gap**
   - Max Gap column highlighted in red/orange for large differences
3. (0:32–0:34) Hover over the highest Max Gap row (e.g., L5 Innovation: 23-point gap)

**Annotation**: Red highlight on Max Gap column: `Cultural disagreement, quantified`

**Voice-over cue**: "The difference table quantifies where cultures disagree — and by how much."

---

### [0:34–0:38] Switch Cultural Tradition

**Screen**: Tradition selector dropdown

**Action**:
1. (0:34–0:35) Click tradition selector at top
2. (0:35–0:36) Switch from "default" to "Japanese Ukiyo-e"
3. (0:36–0:38) Scores visibly shift:
   - Hokusai scores jump up (home tradition advantage)
   - Dunhuang Context score drops
   - Radar chart polygons reshape in real-time

**Annotation**: `Switch tradition → Scores transform`

**Voice-over cue**: "Switch the cultural lens. Watch every score change. Same art. Different truth."

---

### [0:38–0:40] Closing

**Screen**: Radar chart with reshaped polygons after tradition switch

**Action**: Hold on the transformed radar chart

**Annotation**:
- Center (large text): `Same artwork. Different cultures. Different scores.`
- Subtitle: `One standard cannot evaluate all cultures.`
- Bottom: `vulcaart.art` + `Open Source`

**Voice-over cue**: "One standard cannot evaluate all cultures. VULCA understands that."

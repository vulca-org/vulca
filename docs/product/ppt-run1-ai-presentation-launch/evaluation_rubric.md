# Evaluation Rubric

Each dimension receives an integer score from zero through five for the prompt-only baseline and the Vulca case-pack deck.

Score guide: zero means absent or harmful, one means weak, two means partial, three means adequate, four means strong, and five means launch-grade for this run. Each score must include one evidence sentence tied to source notes, generated artifact manifest, contact sheet review, layout JSON, PPTX integrity result, or Gemini critique.

## Dimensions

1. Commercial clarity: Does the deck make the buyer problem, promise, and product value obvious?
2. Narrative flow: Does the sequence create a persuasive movement from problem to decision?
3. Technical understandability: Are AI workflow claims concrete, legible, and free of vague magic?
4. Visual hierarchy: Can a viewer identify the main claim, support, and proof object quickly?
5. Brand coherence: Does the deck feel like one original product system rather than mixed templates?
6. Cultural/design intent: Does the deck show a deliberate design point of view grounded in the references without copying them?
7. Slide-to-slide consistency: Do spacing, type, color, and composition rules stay coherent across all ten slides?
8. Editability: Are text, labels, diagrams, matrices, and key shapes editable in PowerPoint?
9. Accessibility: Are contrast, type size, reading order, label clarity, and density reasonable?
10. Cross-platform rendering risk: Are there features likely to break in PowerPoint, Keynote, Google Slides, or artifact-tool previews?

## Run 1 Rule

Gemini review is evidence, not final authority. A public-facing claim requires human review after generation, repair, and scoring.

Business goal: prove that a self-contained case pack can convert source analysis and tutorial guidance into strict design memory that materially changes code-generated PPT output.

Must-win decision: decide whether Run 1.5 demonstrates a credible wedge for evidence-backed PPT generation or whether the workflow still produces a generic GPT-generated explanatory deck.

Constraints: no copied reference visuals, no copied layouts, no logos, no screenshots, no generated evidence claims before the run, no public publishing before QA gates pass, and no post-training claim.

Desired tone: controlled, commercial, product-lab precise, visually confident, and honest about not-run status.

Output promise: produce a 10-slide editable PPT experiment deck with prompt-only, full Vulca, and bad-data arms ready for comparison and review.

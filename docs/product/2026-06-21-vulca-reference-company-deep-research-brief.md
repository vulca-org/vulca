## Current Positioning Lock

VULCA's current customer-facing wedge should be:

> VULCA is a release-evidence workflow for AI-assisted and high-frequency
> visual assets. It turns source context, visible claims or generated-output
> context, visual annotations, review questions, owner routing, and human
> release gates into source-backed evidence packs before assets move into
> public launch, ecommerce, campaign, marketplace, or client-review workflows.

This is a narrower and more customer-readable form of the older product thesis:
agent-native visual control, decomposition, provider routing, evaluation, and
artifact provenance. The customer should not need to understand the whole
technical stack first. They should first recognize the operational problem:
visual assets now move faster than the source trail, review notes, and release
ownership around them.

Allowed wording:

- evidence-assisted visual review
- source-backed evidence pack
- public example
- human-reviewed release gate
- visual release-evidence workflow
- review packet / handoff packet / evidence card

Do not claim:

- VULCA certifies legal, regulatory, platform, model-safety, or rights status.
- A named public company is a VULCA customer, partner, endorser, authorization
  source, or negative finding.
- A generated or public asset is safe to publish without qualified human review.
- Vendor case-study metrics are VULCA proof.

## Why Study These Companies

The goal is not to copy their products. The goal is to learn how mature
companies turn a fuzzy market need into an operational object that customers
can budget for.

Use this lens for each reference company:

```text
customer pain
-> operating object
-> workflow owner
-> measurable business consequence
-> human/AI boundary
-> what VULCA should borrow
-> what VULCA must avoid claiming
```

The companies below form three reference groups:

1. **Creative quality and creative operations:** CreativeX, Adobe GenStudio.
2. **Review, proofing, and approval gates:** Ziflow, PageProof.
3. **AI visual production at scale:** Creatify, Photoroom, V7.

Shutterstock and Getty are not product-shape references for VULCA, but they are
useful financial/market anchors for why source rights, licensed content,
metadata, and visual data remain economically important in the AI era.

## Deep Pass 2: AI Ad And Ecommerce Visual Production Cluster

This cluster should lead the next narrative pass because it is closest to the
current customer PDF's commercial case pages. The pattern is clear: AI ad and
ecommerce visual companies are proving that visual production can be compressed
from weeks or days into minutes, and scaled across catalogs, channels, sellers,
audiences, and markets. That creates the exact release-evidence gap VULCA
should own.

The strategic lesson is:

```text
AI visual production is becoming fast, embedded, and high-volume
-> review context, source traceability, product fidelity, and campaign handoff become the bottleneck
-> VULCA packages the evidence around the generated or edited asset before release
```

### What This Cluster Changes In The VULCA Story

The older risk was that VULCA sounded like a general visual AI tool. This
cluster makes the sharper story obvious:

```text
Generation tools answer: how do we produce more ads/product visuals faster?

```

## Current One-Paragraph Narrative

Mature companies do not start by saying "we have AI." CreativeX turns creative
quality into a KPI, Ziflow and PageProof turn creative review into checklists,
proof stages, and accountable approvals, while Creatify and Photoroom turn
product inputs into high-volume generated or edited commerce assets. VULCA's
opening should follow the same discipline: customers do not lack another visual
tool; they lack a source-backed release-evidence workflow that records where an
asset came from, what it claims or represents, what still needs review, who owns
the decision, and what can safely move into a bounded human review gate.

## AI Ad / Ecommerce Visual Narrative After Deep Pass 2

AI ad and ecommerce visual platforms are proving that production is no longer
the only bottleneck: product URLs can become video ads, product photos can be
transformed at catalog scale, and brand content systems can generate many
localized variants. VULCA should sit at the next operational layer. Once those
assets exist, VULCA turns source inputs, generated candidates, product-fidelity
questions, claim context, channel/export state, and owner routing into a
reviewable release-evidence packet.

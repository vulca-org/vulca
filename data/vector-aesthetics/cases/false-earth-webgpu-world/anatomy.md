# Anatomy: False Earth

Primitive: a world-scale technical UI sculpture where procedural grass, cosmic beams, interaction waves, and flower lifecycle points form one reviewable WebGPU-era landscape.

Technique: split the world into bounded visual systems: terrain scaffold, grass compute field, beam shader layer, raycast wave proxy, and flower lifecycle markers.

Technique stack:

- Terrain scaffold: a curved horizon and perspective field grid make the world legible as a navigable surface.
- Grass field: repeated bent strokes teach procedural blade shape, wind sway, and density without claiming the original compute shader.
- Cosmic beams: translucent vertical cones provide a shader-material layer and mark the world as uncanny rather than naturalistic.
- Interaction wave: rings on the field stand in for raycast or hover feedback over the ground plane.
- Flower lifecycle: small colored bloom points show spawn/grow/die events without copying VAT frames or upstream assets.

Construction sequence:

1. Draw a dark technical sky and curved false horizon.
2. Add translucent shader beams before foreground detail.
3. Lay a field grid over the ground so perspective and runtime scale can be reviewed.
4. Add 48 procedural grass blades with varied bend and height.
5. Add five wave rings and 12 flower blooms as explicit interaction/lifecycle markers.
6. Label the local evidence and the compute/raycast proxies so the approximation boundary is visible.

Evidence boundary: the canonical Codrops article is retained as source-link-only. PNG, GIF, and HTML are local generated learning evidence and do not claim the upstream WebGPU compute shaders, indirect drawing, VAT frames, raycast code, models, textures, or camera paths.
